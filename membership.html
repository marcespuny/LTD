Membership


LTX has a multi-tiered membership structure.
LTX is open to all companies that meet the relevant criteria, as established by the Exchange's Rules and Regulations.

Categories of Membership
LTX has 5 categories of membership

Category 1 members or floor traders are entitled to trade on the Floor, LTX-Marketplace and the Telephone Market.
Floor traders can issue LTX contracts – buying and selling material for their clients. They are also members of the clearing house LTX-Clear.

Category 2 members or associate broker clearing members have all the privileges of floor traders except they may not trade on the floor.
Category 2 members are entitled to issue LTX contracts and can trade on LTX-Marketplace and the Telephone Market.
They are also members of the clearing house.

Category 3 members can clear their own business but can't issue client contracts or trade on the floor.
Trade clearing members are often industrial or financial companies with key interest in the securities markets.
Category 4 members may issue contracts but are not members of the clearing house.
Category 5 members have no trading rights except as clients.

Becoming a member
Category 1 to 5 membership of the LTX is on a corporate basis and all membership applications must be approved by the Board of the London Trading Exchange. Prior to this, all membership applications undergo a due diligence exercise, whereby the application is assessed by the Exchange. Once an applicant has successfully passed the due diligence exercise, the application will be presented to the Executive Committee in order for the membership application to be endorsed. Should the Executive Committee endorse the membership application, it is presented to the Board of the London Trading Exchange for approval.

Privileges

Membership Fees
LME fees are set annually by the Exchange and agreed by its Board. All fees are standard across membership category or service and apply only to LME members and users of LME systems. Fees charged by LME members to clients are subject to normal commercial negotiation.
