Exchange Services


LTX offers trading and post-trading services to all members.

As of January 2014 LTX also offers Physical Delivery of certain products including Gold and Silver together with Warehousing Services.

Trading
Contract types including futures, options, TAPOs, swaps and LTXX can be traded via Electronic, Floor Trading and Telephone Dealing Monday to Friday 8.00 am – 4.30 pm.
Pre-open routine 7.30 am – 8.00 am and post-close routine 4.30 pm to 5.00 pm.
No trading on bank holidays.


Bid Sizes
Securities are traded in board lot variations (i.e. 1 board lot for Gold would be 100 ounces and 1 board lot for a stock would be 1000 shares). LTX offers bid sizes ranging from +/-10 to +/-20 bids across all price ranges.
Note that Brokerage rates are fully negotiable for all transactions on LTX Securities Trading.

For trading on LTX, the following fees are payable by LTX Members.

- A Clearing Fee of 0.04% on the value of the contract, subject to a maximum of £600 (except for structured warrants which is at 0.05% of the contract value subject to a cap of £200)

- A Trading Fee of 0.0075% on the value of the contract.

VAT applicable on all transactions.

Incentive Schemes, which may include waivers of Clearing Fees (partially or fully), are awarded to market makers and active traders of specific products so as to provide additional liquidity for the benefit of the market.

No dividend tax or withholding tax on dividends paid to non-residents. However, tax at the prevailing corporate tax rate is deducted from the gross dividends payable.

No foreign exchange controls or capital gains tax on the trading of securities by non-residents. There are no restrictions on the acquisition of securities by foreign investors. Foreign ownership is restricted only in certain companies. There are also no limitations on the repatriation of income, capital and capital gains.

No restrictions exist on the acquisition of foreign securities by residents but dividends received from abroad are subject to income tax.


Depository
LTX Clear provides integrated clearing, settlement and depository facilities covering all securities.
LTX Clear is a fully owned subsidiary of the London Trading Exchange.

LTX Clear primarily serves settlements made in the UK, however, has links with other centralised depositories in the US, Japan, China, Malaysia and Singapore to support settlement cross border trades.
The physical certificates of immobilized instruments are kept with a LTX nominated custodian bank.
Depository services provided by LTX Clear include:

1) Statements to Account Holders
2) Deposit of Securities
3) Withdrawal of Securities
4)Transfer of Securities
5) Entitlement Processing and Corporate Actions
6) Securities Lending and Borrowing


Settlement
Trades executed on LTX are required to be settled on T+3. Each trade is settled on a gross basis during an end-of-day settlement run. During the run, securities are transferred from a seller's securities account to that of the buyer and vice versa. LTX Clear uses highly efficient systems to deal with high volumes of operations.

- Front End Clearing Systems (FEC-LTX) includes Forwards Processing and Average Pricing Calculations
- Clear Port Clearing includes Give-up Payment Systems (GPS-LTX) and Broker Payment Systems (BPS-LTX)

All systems include Money Calculations for Futures and Options


Fees
LTX fees are set annually by the exchange and agreed by its board. All fees are standard across membership category or service and apply only to LTX members and users of LTX systems. Fees charged by LTX members to clients are subject to normal commercial negotiation.

Novation
LTX arranges for novation of contracts on settlement. Each member will have individual contracts with the exchange after the novation process in complete.
All novation requests are handled by LTX Clear.
